# Writing Style

## Purpose

This document defines how YSWORKS should write in the public website repository.

Writing must be public-safe, factual, and engineering-led. It must not reveal private systems or invent credibility.

## Tone

The tone should be:

- Clear.
- Calm.
- Technical.
- Direct.
- Professional.
- Precise.

The site should sound like an experienced engineer explaining what is true.

## Language

Use plain language.

Prefer:

- Specific nouns.
- Active voice.
- Concrete outcomes.
- Short explanations.
- Public facts.

Avoid:

- Hype.
- Buzzwords.
- Unsupported claims.
- Dense abstractions.
- Insider language.
- Private implementation details.

## Sentence Length

Keep sentences short.

Most sentences should be under 20 words. Longer sentences are acceptable when they improve precision, but they should be rare.

Use paragraphs of one to three sentences.

## Terminology

Use terms consistently:

- `YSWORKS` for the studio.
- `YSWORKS Web` for this public website repository.
- `public website` for the repository scope.
- `engineering studio` for the public business category.

Avoid internal system names and private project terminology.

## Capitalization

- Write `YSWORKS` in uppercase.
- Use sentence case for headings unless a page design requires another pattern.
- Capitalize product and framework names correctly: Astro, TypeScript, React, Tailwind CSS, Biome, Cloudflare Pages, GitHub.
- Do not capitalize generic concepts for emphasis.

## Forbidden Marketing Language

Avoid words and phrases such as:

- Revolutionary.
- Disruptive.
- World-class.
- Game-changing.
- Cutting-edge.
- Unparalleled.
- Industry-leading.
- Next-generation.
- Best-in-class.
- Transform your business.
- Powered by proprietary AI.
- Autonomous agents.
- Secret system.

Do not write claims that require private evidence or external proof unless the proof can be published.

## Claims

Every claim should be:

- Public.
- Verifiable.
- Maintainable.
- Specific.
- Free of private implementation detail.

Do not invent clients, achievements, metrics, partnerships, certifications, or case studies.

## AI Language

Artificial intelligence should be treated as an implementation detail.

Write about outcomes, not internal mechanisms. Do not reference prompts, agents, private workflows, internal automation, or proprietary architectures.
