# Proposal Template

- Status: governed template
- Owner: named proposal owner
- Classification: client-confidential when completed

## Use

Complete every applicable section for one prospective engagement. Remove
inapplicable guidance before issue. Do not invent proof, capability, timing, or
commercial terms. A proposal is not an authorisation to start work until the
required agreement, approval, and payment conditions are satisfied.
This template applies the [Company Bible](../COMPANY_BIBLE.md)
(*I.III.2; I.IV.1–2,6; I.VIII.2; I.IX.2*) and the
[Client Experience Constitution](../CLIENT_EXPERIENCE_CONSTITUTION.md)
(*III.IV.3–4; III.V.1–4; III.XII.1–3*).

## 2. Context

Describe the client's current situation, affected people, known systems,
constraints, and the evidence gathered during discovery.

Separate verified facts from assumptions and unresolved questions.

## 3. Shared Understanding

State the business need in plain language and explain why it matters. Record the
intended outcome without prescribing technology prematurely.

## 4. Proposed Engagement

Describe the smallest responsible engagement that can produce the intended
outcome or useful evidence.

Include:

- service category;
- delivery approach;
- planned phases;
- principal outputs;
- decision and review points; and
- how documentation and knowledge transfer are included.

## 5. Scope

### Included

List bounded activities and deliverables.

### Excluded

List material exclusions, including services, environments, integrations,
content, migration, support, or legal responsibility not included.

### Assumptions

Record conditions on which scope, timing, or effort depends.

## 8. Security, Privacy, And Access

Describe applicable information classes, data minimisation, access method,
environments, retention, privacy roles, and security review. Link to a separate
approved schedule when detail should not appear in the proposal.

## 10. Communication And Governance

Define:

- authorised contacts and approvers;
- working and formal channels;
- planned reporting or meeting cadence;
- decision and change-control method;
- risk and incident escalation; and
- record ownership.

## 11. Commercial Terms

Reference the accompanying [Quotation](quotation-template.md) and approved legal
agreement. State payment events, taxes, expenses, validity, and commencement
conditions without exposing internal costs or margins.

## 12. Support And Maintenance

State the included post-delivery boundary. If continuing maintenance is
proposed, identify the adaptable level from the
[Maintenance Policy](maintenance-policy.md) and define engagement-specific
availability and responsibility.

## 14. Change Control

Changes to outcome, scope, assumption, dependency, responsibility, timing,
security, or deliverables require a recorded impact assessment and authorised
decision. No informal request silently changes the agreement.

## 15. Approval

Identify the exact proposal reference and version being accepted, the authorised
parties, acceptance method, and timestamp. Acceptance of a proposal does not
directly deploy, merge, publish, change infrastructure, or execute another
privileged action.

## 16. Open Matters

List unresolved matters that must be decided before agreement, commencement, or
a named phase. Absence of an entry does not convert an unknown into approval.
