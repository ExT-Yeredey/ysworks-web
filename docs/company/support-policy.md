# Support Policy

- Status: company policy
- Owner: named support owner
- Scope: agreed assistance for delivered or maintained systems

## Purpose

Support provides a clear, safe route for questions, incidents, defects, and
service requests. It does not create hidden availability, unrestricted access,
or responsibility outside the agreement.
The constitutional basis is the
[Company Bible](../COMPANY_BIBLE.md) (*I.VI.2; I.VIII.1–3; I.XV.1–3*) and the
[Client Experience Constitution](../CLIENT_EXPERIENCE_CONSTITUTION.md)
(*III.X.1–3; III.XIV.1–4*).

## Support Boundary

Every support arrangement defines:

- covered client, systems, environments, and users;
- authorised requesters;
- support channel and operating period;
- priority definitions;
- acknowledgement and target-review expectations;
- included investigation and correction;
- exclusions and separately chargeable work;
- third-party responsibilities;
- access and information handling;
- incident escalation; and
- maintenance or project relationship.

No public document creates a service-level commitment.

## Request Information

Request the minimum useful information:

- affected system or service;
- observed behaviour and impact;
- when it began and whether it is continuing;
- safe reproduction steps;
- recent authorised changes;
- affected users or operations; and
- available non-sensitive evidence.

Clients must not send passwords, secrets, full private logs, regulated data, or
unnecessary personal information through ordinary support channels.

## Matter Taxonomy

| Matter | Meaning | Required handling |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Query | A question or request for understanding | Clarify the question, provide an evidenced answer, and record reusable knowledge where appropriate |
| Request | A desired change or service action where the agreed system is not broken | Confirm scope and authority, then assess through ordinary delivery or change control |
| Incident | System behaviour that conflicts with the agreed or documented state | Verify impact, assign ownership, contain harm where necessary, and communicate facts and the next review point |
| Critical incident | The business is materially stopped or data and security are in question | Use the agreed urgent path, contain risk, establish authority, preserve evidence, and maintain a clear communication cadence |

Priority within an incident is based on verified impact and urgency, not
requester seniority or message volume. Response, resolution, availability, and
service-level commitments exist only when explicitly agreed; the Constitution
does not define them.

## Support Process

1. verify requester and support entitlement;
2. classify impact, information, and security risk;
3. acknowledge receipt without promising an unverified outcome;
4. assign an accountable owner;
5. investigate with minimum necessary access;
6. contain harm before pursuing optimisation;
7. communicate facts, uncertainty, next action, and review point;
8. obtain change authorisation where required;
9. validate the resolution or safe workaround;
10. document the outcome and update the governing system when learning is
    reusable; and
11. close only when the requester and owner understand the final state.

## Access And Privacy

Support access is narrow, purpose-bound, time-limited, and audited where the
system supports it. Support does not receive global client access by default.
Client-session impersonation is prohibited unless a future domain contract
explicitly approves it with consent and audit.

## Exclusions

Unless explicitly agreed, support excludes:

- continuous monitoring or continuous availability;
- unsupported third-party systems;
- new features or material scope changes;
- recovery where no supported backup exists;
- issues caused by unauthorised changes;
- legal, compliance, accounting, or forensic conclusions; and
- advanced offensive security response.

An exclusion does not justify abandoning a client during risk. YSWORKS explains
the boundary, assists with safe containment where authorised, and proposes a
responsible next step.
