# Project Lifecycle

- Status: company lifecycle policy
- Owner: named project owner
- Scope: bounded engagements from qualification to closure

## Purpose

The lifecycle makes state, ownership, evidence, and transition conditions
explicit. It does not prescribe a single project-management tool or delivery
method.
It applies the [Company Bible](../COMPANY_BIBLE.md)
(*I.III.6; I.IX.2; I.XVI*).

## Lifecycle States

### 1. Candidate

A possible need has been identified. No delivery commitment exists.

**Exit:** qualified for discovery or declined with an appropriate explanation.

### 2. Discovery

The problem, people, constraints, systems, risk, timing, decision process, and
desired outcome are investigated.

**Exit:** sufficient shared understanding to propose, investigate further, refer,
or decline.

### 3. Proposed

Scope, exclusions, responsibilities, evidence, timing, commercial terms,
support, and maintenance are offered for decision.

**Exit:** accepted, revised, expired, or declined.

### 4. Agreed

The required proposal, contract, payment condition, data boundary, and decision
authority are in place.

**Exit:** onboarding completes or a blocker returns the engagement to decision.

### 5. Active

Approved work proceeds through design, implementation, validation, review, and
controlled delivery.

**Exit:** deliverables meet acceptance criteria, the project is formally paused,
or authorised change creates a new baseline.

### 6. Acceptance

The client reviews exact deliverables and evidence. Findings are resolved,
accepted as residual risk, or converted into authorised change.

**Exit:** accepted delivery, rejected delivery with next action, or revised
scope.

### 7. Handover

Ownership, access, documentation, operation, support, maintenance, limitations,
and recovery are transferred as agreed.

**Exit:** the recipient confirms the handover boundary.

### 8. Maintenance Or Evolution

Continuing responsibility follows an approved maintenance service or a new
project. Work does not continue through informal scope drift.

**Exit:** review, new agreement, transfer, or closure.

### 9. Closed

The outcome, final state, evidence, access revocation, information retention,
commercial closure, remaining risks, and lessons are recorded.

Closure does not end confidentiality, retention, security, or legal
responsibilities that survive the engagement.

## Project Record

Every project records:

- identifier and approved name;
- client and tenant boundary where applicable;
- outcome, scope, exclusions, assumptions, and baseline;
- accountable YSWORKS and client owners;
- deliverables, milestones, dependencies, and acceptance evidence;
- status based on verified events;
- decisions, risks, changes, and approvals;
- information, access, security, and privacy classification;
- commercial and support references;
- documentation and handover; and
- closure or next review.

Identifiers exposed to clients must not reveal private source identifiers or
create predictable cross-client enumeration.

## Progress

Progress derives from verified milestone criteria and accepted deliverable
states. If a defensible percentage cannot be calculated, report phase, completed
evidence, blockers, next action, owner, and estimate instead.

## Pause And Cancellation

A project may pause when authority, payment, information, access, client
decision, security, capacity, or a material dependency prevents responsible
work. Record the reason, impact, owner, re-entry condition, access treatment,
and communication.

Cancellation follows the agreement and produces an orderly handover or
containment. It does not justify concealing unfinished work or retaining
unnecessary access.
