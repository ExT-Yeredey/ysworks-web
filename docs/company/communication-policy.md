# Communication Policy

- Status: company policy
- Owner: Founder
- Scope: internal, client, supplier, and public communication

## Purpose

Communication must improve understanding, preserve trust, and leave an
appropriate record. This policy applies the voice and writing principles in the
[Company Bible](../COMPANY_BIBLE.md) (*I.VIII.1–3; I.XV.2–3*) to company work.
Client communication also applies the
[Client Experience Constitution](../CLIENT_EXPERIENCE_CONSTITUTION.md)
(*III.VI.1–5; III.XIII.1–7; III.XIV.1–4*).

The [Operating Manual](../OPERATING_MANUAL.md#3-communication-standards)
governs the shared day-to-day communication procedure.

## Principles

- Communicate facts, assumptions, estimates, decisions, and unknowns distinctly.
- Lead with outcome, impact, responsibility, and next action.
- Use plain language appropriate to the audience.
- Raise risk, delay, error, or changed assumptions early.
- Keep private mechanisms and unrelated client information out of messages.
- Do not manufacture urgency, certainty, consensus, or approval.
- Record material decisions in the governed system of record.
- Use AI assistance only within approved information and review boundaries.

## Channels

Each engagement defines:

- the ordinary working channel;
- the formal approval and commercial channel;
- the support channel;
- the incident or urgent escalation path;
- authorised participants; and
- the record that remains authoritative.

A convenient channel does not become authoritative by repeated use. Verbal and
informal decisions that affect scope, cost, risk, approval, or delivery are
confirmed in the agreed record.

## Response Expectations

Acknowledgement and resolution expectations are defined by the engagement or
[Support Policy](support-policy.md). YSWORKS does not imply continuous
availability, immediate resolution, or a service level that has not been
agreed.

When a complete answer is not available, acknowledge the issue, state what is
known, identify the owner, and give the next review point.

## Client Status

Client-visible status includes:

- verified progress and evidence;
- current phase;
- decisions and approvals required;
- blockers and client dependencies;
- next action and responsible visible party;
- estimated timing with stated uncertainty; and
- relevant change, risk, or limitation.

It excludes internal logs, prompts, agents, workflows, infrastructure, costs,
margins, private discussions, and information about another client.

## Meetings

Meetings have a purpose, required participants, preparation, and desired
decision or output. Record decisions, actions, owners, and dates. Avoid meetings
that merely repeat accessible status.

## Public Communication

Public statements follow the [Brand Book](brand-book.md) and evidence-only
portfolio rules. Client identity, work, logos, metrics, testimonials, or
outcomes require explicit permission and verifiable evidence.

## Confidentiality And Security

Verify audience, destination, attachments, links, and access before sending.
Never send credentials or secrets through ordinary documentation or messaging.
Minimise personal and client data. Use approved secure transfer methods when
sensitive material is necessary.

## AI-Assisted Communication

AI may draft or review communication within an approved data boundary. A human
owner verifies accuracy, tone, confidentiality, commitments, and recipients
before external use. AI does not send material external communication or
represent approval without explicit authority.

## Escalation

Stop and escalate when authority is unclear, a commitment would change, private
information may be exposed, legal or safety meaning is uncertain, or the
communication could materially affect trust.
