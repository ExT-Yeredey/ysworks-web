# Client Onboarding

- Status: governed onboarding policy
- Owner: Founder or explicitly assigned engagement owner
- Scope: agreement to controlled project start

## Purpose

Onboarding turns an approved commercial decision into a safe, understandable,
and operable engagement. It applies the
[Company Bible](../COMPANY_BIBLE.md) (*I.III.2,6; I.V.2; I.VI.2–3; I.XV.3*)
and does not replace a contract, privacy notice, security procedure, or project
plan.

## Entry Conditions

Onboarding begins only when:

- the client and authorised decision-makers are identified;
- the problem, intended outcome, scope, exclusions, and assumptions are
  documented;
- commercial terms and required agreement are accepted;
- payment conditions required before commencement are satisfied;
- responsibilities and communication channels are agreed;
- material data, privacy, security, access, and legal questions are resolved or
  explicitly gated; and
- the YSWORKS engagement owner accepts responsibility.

An email conversation or AI-generated document does not replace required
authority.

## Onboarding Record

Record the minimum information required:

| Area | Required record |
| --- | --- |
| Parties | Contracting names, authorised contacts, and decision roles |
| Outcome | Agreed business and user outcome |
| Scope | Included work, exclusions, assumptions, and dependencies |
| Evidence | Acceptance criteria and required validation |
| Schedule | Planned phases, decision points, and known constraints |
| Responsibility | Client and YSWORKS owners, approvers, and escalation path |
| Communication | Approved channels, cadence, and response expectations |
| Information | Data classification, permitted sharing, retention, and deletion |
| Access | Required systems, least-privilege method, owner, and revocation |
| Delivery | Environments, handover, support, maintenance, and closure intent |

Do not place credentials, secrets, unnecessary personal information, internal
costs, or unrelated client data in the onboarding record.

## Start Meeting

The start meeting confirms shared understanding rather than repeating the
proposal. It covers:

1. intended outcome and success evidence;
2. scope, exclusions, assumptions, and change control;
3. users, risks, constraints, and dependencies;
4. client and YSWORKS responsibilities;
5. approvals and decision timing;
6. communication, documentation, and issue handling;
7. access and information boundaries;
8. delivery, handover, support, and maintenance expectations; and
9. the first reviewable milestone.

## Access And Data

Request access only when required for an approved task. Prefer temporary,
individual, least-privilege access with MFA and audit. Record the owner and
revocation condition. Never request passwords by ordinary email or store client
credentials in repository documentation.

Client information is minimised, classified, and used only for the agreed
purpose. AI tools receive client information only when the tool, account,
classification, and use are approved.

## Exit Gate

Onboarding is complete when the engagement has a confirmed owner, safe
information and access boundaries, an accepted first milestone, and no hidden
blocker to responsible delivery.

If authority, payment, scope, security, or legal conditions remain unresolved,
the project does not silently enter implementation.
