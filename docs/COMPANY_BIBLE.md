# YSWORKS Company Bible

- Status: Founder-governed constitutional narrative
- Scope: Company identity, purpose, philosophy, principles, and direction
- Audience: Founder, collaborators, clients, suppliers, and approved human or AI
  operators
- Language: British English

## 00 Purpose Of This Document

This document defines the enduring narrative truth of YSWORKS. It explains what
the company is, why it exists, how it thinks, what it values, and what it intends
to become.

It is not marketing copy, a commercial proposal, an implementation
specification, or a substitute for a domain contract. It supplies the principles
from which product, brand, design, experience, engineering, security, service,
and operational documents derive.

Founder decisions remain the highest authority. Accepted ADRs govern decisions
inside their stated scope. This Company Bible is the highest-level narrative
source. The [Master Specification](YSWORKS_MASTER_SPEC.md) remains the
highest-level ecosystem and architecture specification. The
[Founder Handbook](FOUNDER_HANDBOOK.md) defines company behaviour.

Where approved documents disagree, the conflict is recorded rather than
silently resolved. Until the appropriate authority decides, YSWORKS uses the
more conservative, truthful, and less-exposing position.

## 01 What YSWORKS Is

YSWORKS is a technology engineering company.

It studies business needs and designs, builds, secures, documents, and maintains
useful digital systems. Its work may combine software, web engineering,
automation, artificial intelligence, infrastructure, professional hardware,
cybersecurity, integration, consulting, and continuing support.

YSWORKS is responsible for the quality of its decisions and the work it agrees
to deliver. Tools may extend capability; they do not replace accountability.

YSWORKS is not a marketing agency, a template website shop, a generic chatbot
provider, a seller of autonomous agents, a no-code reseller, or a public window
into private infrastructure.

## 02 Why YSWORKS Exists

Organisations often accumulate tools, manual work, unclear ownership, fragile
integrations, and systems that are difficult to understand or change. Technology
can increase that burden when it is selected before the business is understood.

YSWORKS exists to reverse that pattern. It turns real needs into systems that are
useful now, understandable by their owners, and prepared to evolve without
unnecessary dependence or concealed complexity.

## 03 Mission

YSWORKS designs, builds, secures, and maintains digital systems that help
organisations operate clearly and reliably. It applies engineering discipline,
automation, and artificial intelligence where they produce a defined,
responsible, and maintainable outcome.

## 04 Vision

Over ten years and beyond, YSWORKS intends to remain a focused and trusted
technology engineering company known for durable systems, direct communication,
careful execution, and long-term client relationships.

Growth must compound capability without weakening quality, security,
maintainability, accessibility, honesty, or human accountability. YSWORKS may
develop public products and deeper internal capabilities, but it will not become
dependent on spectacle, unsupported claims, uncontrolled scale, or opaque
automation.

## 05 Positioning

YSWORKS is the engineering partner for organisations that need digital systems
to work clearly, securely, and sustainably.

The company sells responsible outcomes and systems, not internal tools, agents,
prompts, workflows, or infrastructure. A website, automation, AI capability, or
hardware installation may be part of a system; none defines the company alone.

The brand idea is **Quiet Engineering**: invisible complexity, visible
simplicity. This idea describes the standard of work, not a promise that
complexity or risk can be eliminated.

## 06 Engineering Philosophy

Engineering begins with understanding. YSWORKS identifies the real problem,
affected people, constraints, existing systems, risks, ownership, and evidence
before selecting technology.

The preferred solution is the smallest responsible system that creates useful
value and can be validated, operated, maintained, and changed. Complexity must
earn its place. Every material dependency, automation, integration, data flow,
and public surface requires a clear purpose and owner.

Engineering quality includes correctness, security, privacy, accessibility,
performance, maintainability, observability, recoverability, documentation, and
supportability where applicable.

## 07 Central Operating Principle

Every project must leave the client with a better-understood business, a useful
and documented system, and a maintainable foundation prepared to evolve.

This principle applies whether the outcome is a strategy, specification,
prototype, integration, deployed system, recovery plan, or decision not to
build.

## 08 Official Slogan

**Engineering useful systems.**

The slogan is a concise expression of the company purpose. It does not replace a
service description, evidence, scope, or contractual commitment.

## 09 Company Values

- **Truth:** distinguish fact, evidence, assumption, estimate, and unknown.
- **Responsibility:** assign an accountable human to consequential work.
- **Usefulness:** solve an actual need before optimising presentation.
- **Quality:** define and verify what acceptable means.
- **Security:** reduce exposure and design controls with the system.
- **Clarity:** make decisions and systems understandable to their audience.
- **Maintainability:** respect the next responsible maintainer.
- **Respect:** protect client attention, privacy, autonomy, and informed choice.
- **Restraint:** avoid unjustified scope, claims, dependencies, and complexity.
- **Learning:** turn evidence and mistakes into durable improvements.

## 10 Foundational Principles

### Understand Before Building

Investigation precedes prescription. A request is evidence of a need, not proof
that the requested implementation is correct.

### Technology Follows Understanding

Technology is selected after the outcome, users, constraints, risks, ownership,
and operating conditions are known.

### Useful Beats Impressive

A reliable and understandable system is more valuable than a novel system that
cannot be supported or explained.

### Documentation Is Part Of The Product

Material decisions, boundaries, operation, limitations, and handover information
are deliverables, not administrative residue.

### Human Judgement Remains Accountable

Consequential decisions retain a named human owner. Delegation and automation do
not remove responsibility.

### Systems Evolve Continuously

Systems are designed for controlled change. Evolution follows evidence,
versioned decisions, and explicit ownership.

### Evidence Before Promises

Claims, progress, completion, security, and public proof require proportionate
evidence. Absence of evidence is stated honestly.

### Automation Serves People

Automation reduces avoidable work and improves consistency. It must not obscure
responsibility, remove necessary judgement, or create dependence without value.

### Security Is Designed, Not Added

Identity, access, data boundaries, failure, recovery, privacy, and exposure are
considered from the beginning.

### Quality Compounds Over Time

Clear decisions, reusable standards, maintenance, and learning reduce future
risk and improve delivery.

### Leave Every Business Better

YSWORKS leaves each engagement with greater understanding, useful outputs,
honest records, and an orderly next step, including when the correct outcome is
to stop.

## 11 Personality

YSWORKS is calm, precise, independent, technical, considered, and trustworthy.
It is serious without becoming cold, confident without exaggeration, and
premium through consistency rather than display.

The company does not imitate a large organisation, manufacture social proof, or
perform urgency. Its character is visible in the quality of its boundaries,
writing, decisions, and delivered work.

## 12 Tone Of Voice

Communication is professional, modern, honest, direct, calm, and technically
precise.

YSWORKS uses plain language, specific nouns, active voice, short paragraphs, and
explicit limits. It distinguishes present capability, future direction,
exploration, private systems, and unavailable features.

It avoids buzzwords, manufactured urgency, inflated certainty, vague AI claims,
and language that implies guaranteed outcomes.

## 13 Writing Philosophy

Writing exists to improve understanding and support a decision.

Documents lead with purpose, audience, scope, authority, and outcome. They use
one term for one concept, state exclusions where they matter, separate evidence
from inference, and link to detailed authority instead of copying it.

Public writing is safe for its audience. Client writing is tenant-scoped.
Internal material is never made public by simple redaction or pass-through.

## 14 Design Philosophy

Design makes complex systems understandable without pretending that complexity
does not exist. It is minimal, technical, modern, responsive, and accessible.

Hierarchy, typography, spacing, contrast, and consistency carry more weight than
effects. YSWORKS Purple provides identity with restraint. Detailed tokens and
component rules remain governed by the
[approved design authorities](design/README.md) and
[production design-system contracts](../design-system/README.md).

## 15 Motion Philosophy

Stillness communicates confidence. Motion is rationed and meaningful.

Motion may explain sequence, hierarchy, state, or response. It must not decorate
uncertainty, conceal content, delay access, hijack scrolling, or compete with
the task. Content remains visible and usable without animation or JavaScript,
and reduced-motion preferences are respected.

The Public Website animation decision remains governed by ADR-M004 and the
[motion contract](../design-system/motion.md).

## 16 User Experience Philosophy

The experience should reduce effort, uncertainty, and unnecessary choice. It
begins with the user's question, not the organisation's internal structure.

Public experiences provide clear paths and honest expectations. Client
experiences prioritise the executive view: what matters, what requires action,
what is blocked, what happens next, and which evidence supports the status.
Technical detail is available only when useful and authorised.

Accessibility, performance, responsive behaviour, keyboard operation, clear
errors, and safe failure are product requirements.

## 17 Engineering Standards

Every material engagement defines:

- the problem, intended outcome, owner, and authority;
- users, data, trust boundaries, and affected systems;
- scope, exclusions, assumptions, and acceptance evidence;
- failure, rollback, recovery, operation, support, and maintenance where
  relevant;
- review, validation, release, and change control;
- documentation and handover; and
- known limitations, residual risks, and review triggers.

Implementation remains reviewable and reversible in proportion to risk.
Automated checks provide evidence but do not replace professional judgement.
Detailed standards belong to the applicable product, engineering, or security
contract.

## 18 Security Philosophy

Security protects people, client trust, information, systems, and the ability to
operate. It uses least privilege, separation of public and private systems,
minimal data, explicit trust boundaries, secure defaults, revocable access,
auditable change, and tested recovery.

A service is not safe to expose merely because it has authentication. Private
control planes, data stores, logs, credentials, internal workflows, client
information, and YS AI OS internals remain private.

The [Secure Public Platform Foundation](architecture/SECURE_PUBLIC_PLATFORM_FOUNDATION.md)
and detailed domain contracts remain authoritative for security.

## 19 Documentation Philosophy

Documentation is governed knowledge. Every authoritative document has a purpose,
scope, audience, owner, status, and relationship to higher authority.

YSWORKS writes at the highest useful level once, then cross-references it.
Domain documents add detail without restating company identity. Superseded
guidance is marked or retired. Genuine conflicts remain visible until resolved.

Documentation approval does not authorise a production, infrastructure,
commercial, legal, or client-data change.

## 20 AI Philosophy

AI augments engineering.

AI never replaces responsibility.

AI may assist research, analysis, drafting, implementation, validation, review,
and authorised operations. Its inputs are minimised and classified. Its outputs
are treated as unverified until reviewed in proportion to consequence.

AI does not become an accountable actor, bind YSWORKS or a client, approve
scope or cost, accept legal liability, move money outside approved controls,
publish to production, or bypass privacy, security, audit, and human
authorisation.

YS AI OS is private internal infrastructure. It is not the public brand, a
commercial product, or proof of client value.

## 21 Services

YSWORKS provides bounded engineering services across AI Systems, Automation,
Software Development, Web Development, practical Cybersecurity, Infrastructure,
Systems and Professional Hardware, Consulting, and Maintenance.

Training is a supporting delivery capability, not an independent launch
category. Services are scoped to the client problem and responsible delivery;
they do not imply one technology or implementation.

The authoritative categories, exclusions, and status are defined in
[Master Specification section 3](YSWORKS_MASTER_SPEC.md#3-services). The
[Capability Statement](company/capability-statement.md) provides the concise
company-facing view.

## 22 Client Experience

The enduring client lifecycle is:

**Discovery → Understanding → Proposal → Agreement → Delivery → Documentation → Evolution**

- **Discovery** establishes the problem, people, context, constraints, and
  decision process.
- **Understanding** creates a shared model of the business need, risks, current
  state, and useful outcome.
- **Proposal** defines scope, exclusions, responsibilities, evidence, timing,
  support, and commercial terms.
- **Agreement** records informed authority to begin the bounded engagement.
- **Delivery** produces reviewable work against the agreed acceptance criteria.
- **Documentation** transfers decisions, operation, limitations, and ownership.
- **Evolution** chooses maintenance, a new scoped engagement, orderly closure, or
  no further work.

The lifecycle is direct, low-pressure, traceable, and free of manufactured
urgency or dependence. The detailed operating contracts live in
[Client Onboarding](company/client-onboarding.md),
[Delivery Process](company/delivery-process.md), and
[Project Lifecycle](company/project-lifecycle.md).

## 23 Ecosystem

The public narrative model is:

**YSWORKS → Public Website → Client Workspace → Private Internal Systems**

- **YSWORKS** is the company and sole public commercial identity.
- **Public Website** is the anonymous source of approved company and service
  information.
- **Client Workspace** is the long-term client-facing experience for authorised,
  tenant-isolated project information and collaboration.
- **Private Internal Systems** support YSWORKS operations without becoming
  public or client-visible.

The current architectural contract calls the future authenticated product the
**Client Portal** at `portal.ysworks.dev`. “Client Workspace” is the approved
conceptual direction in this narrative, not an implemented rename. Final
product naming remains an Open Decision.

YS AI OS is internal infrastructure. It is not a public brand and has no
independent commercial identity.

## 24 Client Workspace

The Client Workspace should give a client a calm, authorised, executive-first
view of its relationship with YSWORKS.

Its conceptual areas are Dashboard, Company, Projects, Documentation,
Deliverables, Roadmap, Support, Billing, Marketplace, and Settings. Sections
exist only when backed by a real, authorised capability; empty product theatre
is not published.

Executive information comes first: current state, verified progress, decisions,
risks, dependencies, next action, owner, and evidence. Technical detail is
optional, contextual, and filtered through a safe client projection.

A future AI assistant is approved as a direction only. It must be
tenant-isolated, purpose-bound, transparent about limitations, unable to bypass
authorisation, and subject to human accountability. No assistant, runtime,
authentication, data connection, or portal capability is authorised by this
document.

The [Client Portal Foundation](architecture/CLIENT_PORTAL_FOUNDATION.md) remains
the detailed authority for isolation, permissions, approvals, documents,
privacy, and security.

## 25 Long-Term Roadmap

The roadmap has no dates and creates no delivery commitment:

1. maintain a clear, accessible, and trustworthy Public Website;
2. establish repeatable company, commercial, quality, and delivery standards;
3. prototype the Client Workspace without client data or implied capability;
4. introduce authenticated, read-only, tenant-isolated client projections;
5. add governed approvals, documents, support, and billing only after their
   domain gates are satisfied;
6. strengthen internal automation and knowledge without exposing private
   systems;
7. develop public products only when audience, evidence, security, support, and
   commercial responsibility are approved; and
8. evolve the company through evidence while preserving its constitutional
   principles.

Each stage requires separate scope, authority, validation, and production
approval.

## 26 Decision Making

Material decisions identify:

1. the problem and intended outcome;
2. the affected people, clients, systems, and data;
3. the authority and constraints;
4. evidence, assumptions, and uncertainty;
5. realistic alternatives, including no change;
6. security, privacy, accessibility, maintenance, and support consequences;
7. reversal, containment, and exit paths; and
8. the accountable owner and review trigger.

Prefer the smallest reversible decision that produces useful evidence. Urgency,
technical ability, historical access, silence, or AI recommendation does not
constitute authority.

## 27 Governance Hierarchy

1. Explicit Founder decisions govern company direction and resolve otherwise
   unresolved conflicts when recorded.
2. Accepted ADRs govern the decisions and scope they explicitly address.
3. This Company Bible governs company narrative, identity, purpose, philosophy,
   and enduring principles.
4. The Master Specification governs ecosystem architecture, official technical
   vocabulary, products, and cross-system boundaries.
5. The Founder Handbook governs company behaviour and human–AI collaboration.
6. Detailed security, portal, business, design, engineering, and operational
   contracts govern their stated domains and may impose stricter constraints.
7. Implementation and templates conform to the applicable authorities; they do
   not create policy by existence.

No lower-level document can weaken a higher-level requirement. A domain
contract may be stricter inside its scope. Cross-domain conflicts remain open
until the appropriate authority resolves them.

## 28 Document Hierarchy

| Layer | Purpose | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Constitution | Company narrative and enduring direction | This Company Bible |
| Architecture | Ecosystem, systems, products, and accepted decisions | Master Specification and ADRs |
| Behaviour | Human conduct, judgement, and collaboration | Founder Handbook |
| Domain contract | Security, portal, design, quality, commercial, or delivery rules | Architecture and company policies |
| Procedure | Repeatable bounded method | Onboarding and delivery process |
| Template | Consistent record structure | Proposal and quotation templates |
| Evidence | Proof of a specific decision, validation, or delivery | Approval, test result, handover record |

Documents link upwards and avoid copying higher-level prose. Evidence never
becomes policy merely because it was produced once.

## 29 Glossary

### Agreement

The recorded commercial and delivery authority for a defined engagement.

### Client

An organisation or person with an agreed YSWORKS engagement. In client-facing
systems, one client maps to an isolated tenant.

### Client Portal

The current architectural term for the future authenticated product governed by
the Client Portal Foundation.

### Client Workspace

The conceptual, executive-first client experience described by this Company
Bible. Its final public product name remains undecided.

### Deliverable

A defined, reviewable output tied to an engagement, version, acceptance
criteria, and owner.

### Evidence

A verifiable record that supports a claim, decision, status, validation, or
acceptance.

### Founder

The final authority for company direction and the owner of non-delegated
constitutional decisions.

### Maintenance

An agreed continuing responsibility to keep a system secure, current,
understandable, supported, or improving within explicit boundaries.

### Project

A bounded engagement with an outcome, scope, owner, evidence, lifecycle, and
closure condition.

### System

A purposeful combination of people, process, software, data, infrastructure,
and documentation with defined boundaries and ownership.

### Tenant

The enforced client-isolation boundary in a client-facing system.

### YS AI OS

Private internal infrastructure used to support YSWORKS. It is not a public
brand, public product, or client entitlement.
