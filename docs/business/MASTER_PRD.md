# Master PRD

## Purpose

This PRD defines the public YSWORKS website as a commercial and trust-building
surface.

It does not define private systems or implementation details. It describes what
the public website should eventually communicate and enable.

## Website Objectives

- Explain what YSWORKS is.
- Communicate services clearly.
- Build trust through quality, transparency, and restraint.
- Provide a path to contact.
- Publish public-safe writing and experiments.
- Support future real portfolio, products, and case studies.
- Protect private systems and proprietary knowledge.

## Primary Users

- Founders and startup operators.
- Small and medium business owners.
- Technical leaders.
- Product teams.
- Institutions exploring digital systems.
- Potential collaborators.
- Future clients evaluating trust.

## User Journeys

### First-Time Visitor

1. Lands on the homepage.
2. Understands YSWORKS as an engineering studio.
3. Reviews services or work principles.
4. Checks trust signals.
5. Contacts YSWORKS or explores Journal/Labs.

### Technical Evaluator

1. Reads service or technical content.
2. Looks for evidence of engineering quality.
3. Checks maintainability, security, and public boundaries.
4. Decides whether YSWORKS is credible enough for a conversation.

### Potential Client

1. Identifies a relevant problem.
2. Reviews service category and expected outcomes.
3. Sees clear contact options.
4. Sends inquiry with minimal friction.

## Conversions

Primary conversion:

- qualified contact inquiry.

Secondary conversions:

- reading a service page;
- reading Journal or Labs content;
- returning to review public work;
- joining a future newsletter;
- downloading a future public resource.

## CTA Strategy

Use clear CTAs:

- Contact YSWORKS.
- View Services.
- Explore Work.
- Read Journal.
- Explore Labs.

Avoid vague CTAs such as “Transform your business” or “Unlock AI.”

## Structure

Initial structure:

- Home.
- Services.
- Work.
- Labs.
- Journal.
- About.
- Contact.
- Privacy.

Future structure:

- Service detail pages.
- Real work detail pages.
- Journal articles.
- Labs entries.
- Product pages when products are public.

## Success Criteria

The website succeeds when:

- visitors understand what YSWORKS does within one screen;
- navigation contains no dead ends;
- service pages are specific but public-safe;
- contact path is clear;
- SEO basics are correct;
- accessibility and performance are strong;
- no fake proof appears;
- private systems remain private.

## KPIs

Use quality metrics before aggressive growth metrics:

- Core Web Vitals.
- Lighthouse accessibility score.
- valid metadata and sitemap.
- no broken internal links.
- contact conversion rate.
- qualified inquiry rate.
- useful page depth.
- returning technical readers.
- content freshness.

Do not optimize for vanity traffic at the expense of trust.
