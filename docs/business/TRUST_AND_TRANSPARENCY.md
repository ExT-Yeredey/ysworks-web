# Trust And Transparency

## Purpose

Trust is a product requirement for YSWORKS Web.

Trust should come from engineering quality, clarity, documentation, accessibility,
security, and consistency.

## Principles

- Tell the truth.
- Do not exaggerate.
- Do not invent clients.
- Do not invent metrics.
- Do not invent partnerships.
- Do not publish fake testimonials.
- Do not show private technology.
- Do not imply work is complete when it is exploratory.

## How To Generate Trust

Use:

- fast pages;
- accessible UI;
- clear service boundaries;
- public-safe documentation;
- real writing;
- real work when approved;
- transparent limitations;
- stable navigation;
- clear contact expectations.

## What Not To Do

Avoid:

- fake urgency;
- fake scarcity;
- stock proof;
- AI hype;
- unsupported claims;
- hidden data collection;
- broken links;
- placeholder proof.

## Transparency Patterns

Use direct statements:

- “Case study pending approval.”
- “Labs content is exploratory.”
- “YS AI OS remains private.”
- “This page describes public services, not internal implementation.”

## Commercial Trust

A potential client should understand:

- what YSWORKS can help with;
- what YSWORKS will not fake;
- how to start a conversation;
- what information is needed;
- what remains private.

## Technical Trust

The website should demonstrate:

- correct metadata;
- no dead routes;
- strong accessibility;
- low JavaScript;
- security headers when deployed;
- clean documentation;
- no secrets.
