# Lead Generation

## Purpose

Lead generation should support qualified conversations without growth hacking.

The website should make it easy for the right visitor to contact YSWORKS and
easy for the wrong visitor to self-filter.

## Funnel

1. Visitor understands positioning.
2. Visitor finds relevant service or content.
3. Visitor sees trust principles.
4. Visitor chooses a clear CTA.
5. Visitor submits or sends inquiry.
6. YSWORKS qualifies fit.

## CTAs

Recommended CTAs:

- Contact YSWORKS.
- View Services.
- Explore Work.
- Read Journal.
- Explore Labs.

Avoid CTAs that promise transformation, instant results, or unsupported AI
capability.

## Forms

A public form should be added only after privacy and abuse protection are ready.

Requirements:

- Cloudflare Turnstile;
- honeypot field;
- rate limiting;
- server-side validation;
- minimal fields;
- privacy notice;
- generic success and error messages;
- no exposure of private routing.

## Newsletter

A newsletter may exist later if there is enough durable content.

It should be:

- low frequency;
- technical;
- public-safe;
- easy to unsubscribe from;
- free of spam.

## Contact

Before a form exists, the contact page should provide a safe contact path and
explain what information helps qualify a request.

Collect only what is needed.

## Downloads

Downloads may be useful later:

- checklists;
- technical briefs;
- public templates;
- audit guides.

Do not gate content aggressively before trust exists.

## Labs And Journal

Labs and Journal should support lead generation indirectly by showing thinking,
quality, and taste.

They should not become a content farm.

## Conversion Quality

Measure:

- qualified inquiries;
- service page engagement;
- contact starts;
- content returning visitors.

Do not optimize for raw traffic alone.
