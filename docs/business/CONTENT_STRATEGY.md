# Content Strategy

## Purpose

Content should build trust through clarity, usefulness, and restraint.

It must never reveal private systems or invent credibility.

## What To Publish

- service explanations;
- public-safe technical notes;
- accessibility and performance learnings;
- product thinking;
- Labs experiments;
- approved work summaries;
- open-source notes when real;
- public product documentation when products exist.

## What Not To Publish

- private architecture;
- YS AI OS implementation details;
- prompts;
- agents as internal mechanisms;
- MCP servers;
- workflows;
- infrastructure;
- private business logic;
- fake case studies;
- fake metrics;
- speculative roadmaps;
- confidential client material.

## How To Show Work

Use real, approved work only.

A work page may include:

- problem;
- constraints;
- public-safe role;
- outcome;
- technical themes;
- what can be shown;
- what remains private.

If a case study is not ready, use work principles instead of placeholder case
cards.

## How To Protect Intellectual Property

- Describe outcomes, not private methods.
- Avoid diagrams of internal systems.
- Avoid screenshots containing sensitive data.
- Review client permissions before publishing.
- Remove implementation details that create competitive exposure.

## How To Talk About YS AI OS

YS AI OS may be described only as an internal platform that helps YSWORKS improve
quality, leverage, documentation, and execution.

Do not describe:

- architecture;
- agents;
- prompts;
- workflows;
- infrastructure;
- internal automations;
- security model;
- private roadmap.

## How To Talk About Agents

Use public language such as:

- AI-assisted engineering;
- automation support;
- internal tooling;
- controlled workflows.

Avoid presenting private agents as public products until a public product exists.

## How To Talk About Products

Products should be published only when:

- they exist in a public-ready form;
- scope is approved;
- privacy and security are reviewed;
- support expectations are clear;
- no private systems are exposed.
